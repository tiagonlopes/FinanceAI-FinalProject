5. Methodology Notes

  • Data Sources: Technical and fundamental data are sourced from the provided input as of 2026-06-09.
  • Forecast Models: Short-term (5-day) price forecasts are generated using two time-series models:
    • ARIMA (AutoRegressive Integrated Moving Average): A statistical model that uses historical data patterns to forecast future values.
    • Prophet: A forecasting tool developed by Meta, designed for time-series data with strong seasonal components.
    • Ensemble Forecast: The primary forecast is an average of ARIMA and Prophet models.
  • Forecast Agreement: "Converging" implies both models largely agree on the direction and magnitude, increasing confidence. "Diverging" indicates significant disagreement, suggesting higher forecast uncertainty. "Neutral" indicates moderate agreement.
  • Bullish Probability (5-day): A rule-based technical score, combining multiple technical indicators (e.g., RSI, MACD, Bollinger Bands, SMAs, trend) to quantify short-term bullish likelihood. This score is purely technical and does not incorporate fundamental factors.
  • Valuation Ratios: P/E (Trailing & Forward) and PEG ratios are compared against sector averages and earnings growth to assess relative valuation.
  • Limitations: This report is based on a single snapshot of data and short-term forecasts, which are inherently volatile and subject to rapid change.
