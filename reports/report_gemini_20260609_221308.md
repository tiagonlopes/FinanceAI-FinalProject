## 5. Methodology Notes

*   **Data Sources:** Technical indicators, fundamental metrics, and price data are sourced as of 2026-06-09.
*   **Forecast Models:**
    *   **ARIMA (AutoRegressive Integrated Moving Average):** A statistical model for time series forecasting, suitable for capturing trends and seasonality based on historical data.
    *   **Prophet:** A forecasting tool developed by Meta, optimized for business forecasting tasks, handling seasonality, holidays, and missing data well.
    *   **Ensemble Model:** The primary 5-day forecast is an average (mean) of the ARIMA and Prophet model outputs, aiming to leverage the strengths of both.
*   **Forecast Agreement:** "Converging" implies that ARIMA and Prophet models generally agree on the direction and magnitude of the short-term price movement, increasing confidence in the ensemble forecast. "Diverging" indicates significant disagreement, suggesting higher uncertainty and caution in interpreting the ensemble. "Neutral" falls between these extremes.
*   **Bullish Probability:** A rule-based technical score (0-100%) indicating the likelihood of a bullish move over the next 5 days, derived from a proprietary combination of technical indicators.
*   **Caveats and Limitations:**
    *   Forecasts are short-term (5-day outlook) and are based on historical price and technical data, which may not predict future performance.
    *   Fundamental data provides a snapshot and does not account for forward-looking changes in company performance or macroeconomics.
    *   Market conditions can change rapidly, rendering short-term forecasts and technical signals quickly outdated.
    *   The "bullish probability" is a technical-only score and may not capture fundamental shifts or broader market sentiment.
