5. Methodology Notes

Data Sources: The analysis is based on the technical and fundamental data explicitly provided in the prompt. All prices, ratios, growth rates, and indicator values are taken directly from the input.

Forecast Models: Two distinct time series forecasting models are employed: 1. ARIMA (AutoRegressive Integrated Moving Average): This is a class of models that captures various standard temporal structures in time series data. It is particularly effective at modeling linear relationships and trends in the data. 2. Prophet: Developed by Facebook, Prophet is an additive model designed for forecasting univariate time series data with strong seasonal effects, holiday effects, and different trend changes. It is robust to missing data and shifts in the trend.

An Ensemble (mean) forecast is generated by averaging the predictions from the ARIMA and Prophet models. This approach aims to reduce the bias and variance of individual models, potentially leading to a more robust forecast when the models show reasonable agreement.

Forecast Agreement: The level of "forecast agreement" is assessed by comparing the projected directional change and magnitude between the ARIMA and Prophet models. * Converging: Implies both models agree on the direction and have relatively similar magnitudes of change, increasing confidence in the ensemble forecast. * Neutral/Moderate: Indicates some agreement on direction but potentially different magnitudes, leading to moderate confidence. * Diverging: Signifies disagreement in direction or substantial differences in magnitude, leading to "high uncertainty" in the ensemble forecast. In such cases, the ensemble average should be interpreted with caution, as it attempts to reconcile conflicting signals.
