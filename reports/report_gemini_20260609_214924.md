## 5. Methodology Notes

**Data Sources:** The analysis is based on financial and technical data provided as of 2026-06-09. All prices, ratios, growth rates, and indicator values are direct inputs from the provided dataset.

**Forecast Models:**
Price forecasts are generated using two primary time-series models:
1.  **ARIMA (AutoRegressive Integrated Moving Average):** A classical statistical method effective for capturing linear trends, seasonality, and autoregressive components in time series data. It generally performs well on stable datasets.
2.  **Prophet (by Facebook):** A procedure for forecasting time series data based on an additive model where non-linear trends are fit with yearly, weekly, and daily seasonality, plus holiday effects. It is robust to missing data and shifts in trends and typically handles datasets with strong seasonal effects well.

**Ensemble Forecast:** The "Ensemble (mean) 5-day" forecast is calculated as the simple average of the ARIMA and Prophet 5-day price projections. This approach aims to leverage the strengths of both models and potentially reduce the risk of over-reliance on a single model.

**Forecast Agreement/Disagreement:**
-   **Converging:** Implies both ARIMA and Prophet models are predicting similar directional movements and magnitudes, increasing confidence in the ensemble forecast.
-   **Neutral:** Suggests moderate agreement, with models showing similar direction but some difference in magnitude, leading to moderate confidence.
-   **Diverging:** Indicates significant disagreement between ARIMA and Prophet regarding the future price path and/or magnitude of movement. This signals high uncertainty and necessitates extreme caution when interpreting the ensemble forecast.

**Rule-Based Technical Probability:** The "Bullish probability (5-day, rule-based technical score)" is an aggregated score derived from a set of predefined technical analysis rules and indicators. It provides a directional lean (bullish/bearish) for the next 5 days, based purely on technical inputs.

**Caveats and Limitations:**
-   **Forward-Looking Statements:** Forecasts are inherently uncertain and are not guarantees of future performance. Market conditions can change rapidly.
-   **Model Assumptions:** Both ARIMA and Prophet models operate on certain assumptions about data stationarity, seasonality, and trend. Violations of these assumptions can impact forecast accuracy.
-   **Data Scope:** The analysis is limited to the provided dataset. Additional qualitative factors, macroeconomic events, news sentiment, or alternative data sources not included here could alter the outlook.
-   **Technical vs. Fundamental:** Technical analysis primarily focuses on short-term price movements and patterns, while fundamental analysis assesses the long-term intrinsic value and financial health of a company. A comprehensive investment decision should integrate both.
-   **Benchmark Context:** Benchmark ETFs (SPY, QQQ, DIA, IWM) are used solely to gauge broader market sentiment and trends and are not subjected to the same valuation or sentiment analysis as individual stocks.
-   **Dividend Yield Anomalies:** Some dividend yield figures, particularly for ETFs, appear unusually high and may indicate data entry errors or specific calculation methodologies not fully explained.
