## 5. Methodology Notes

This investment research report is based on quantitative analysis of provided market data as of 2026-06-09.

**Data Sources:** All technical and fundamental data points used in this report are derived directly from the provided dataset.

**Forecast Models:**
*   **ARIMA (AutoRegressive Integrated Moving Average):** A statistical model that uses past values to predict future values in a time series. It is effective for capturing linear relationships and trends in data.
*   **Prophet:** A forecasting tool developed by Facebook, optimized for business forecasting tasks. It handles seasonality, holidays, and missing data robustly, often excelling with longer-term trends and non-linear patterns.
*   **Ensemble (Mean):** The ensemble forecast is calculated as the arithmetic mean of the ARIMA and Prophet model outputs. This approach aims to leverage the strengths of both models and provide a more robust prediction, though its reliability is directly tied to the agreement between the constituent models.

**Forecast Agreement:**
*   **Converging:** Implies both ARIMA and Prophet models are moving in the same direction, reinforcing the forecast and increasing confidence.
*   **Neutral/Moderate:** Suggests some alignment but with differences in magnitude, indicating reasonable confidence but acknowledging potential variability.
*   **Diverging:** Signifies that ARIMA and Prophet models are predicting significantly different outcomes (direction or magnitude). This leads to *high uncertainty* in the ensemble forecast, as the models offer conflicting signals. Such divergence often calls for caution and indicates that the market's future path may be highly unpredictable or subject to rapid shifts.

**Caveats and Limitations:**
*   **Quantitative Focus:** This report relies exclusively on the provided quantitative data. Qualitative factors, company-specific news, macroeconomic events, and geopolitical developments are not incorporated unless explicitly reflected in the numerical data.
*   **Short-Term Horizon:** The 5-day forecasts are inherently short-term and should not be extrapolated for long-term investment decisions.
*   **Model Limitations:** While robust, forecasting models like ARIMA and Prophet have inherent limitations. They are based on historical data and assumptions that may not hold true in future market conditions. Unexpected events ("black swans") are not accounted for.
*   **Rule-Based Probability:** The "bullish probability" score is a rule-based technical indicator, solely reflecting patterns in price, volume, and momentum. It does not incorporate fundamental analysis or external factors.
*   **Forward-Looking Statements:** All forecasts and sentiment assessments are forward-looking and inherently speculative. Actual results may differ materially.
